LONG ISLAND CITY, NEW YORK

LONG ISLAND CITY, NY

While it may seem to have a misleading name, Long Island City is no longer considered an actual city, as the district is now a bustling neighborhood that occupies the furthest western tip of the Queens borough of New York City. It was founded and incorporated in 1870 from a merger between the Village of Astoria and eight neighboring hamlets and was the seat of the Town of Newtown before Queens was annexed to New York City. When that happened in 1898, Long Island City lost its independence and became a part of Queens. Much of the cities’ history was spent as an industrial district, but as of the early 21st century, the neighborhood was rezoned for commercial and residential use, and the area has become well-known for ongoing, growth.


In 2001, Long Island City was officially changed from an industrial district to a residential neighborhood. With that came building new developments, such as Hunter’s Point South. The district’s growth continued through the following years, and between 2010 and 2017, an impressive 41 new apartment buildings were built. As of the mid-2010s, Long Island City has become one of the fastest-growing neighborhoods in New York City, not only architecturally but also population-wise, as “L.I.C.” saw thousands of new residents arrive looking to make the region their new home.

 

The neighborhood has become a highly desirable place to live and a destination for people looking to have a good time. The district has no shortage of entertainment options. Many abandoned industrial buildings have been repurposed and put to good use; for example, the MoMA PS1, an affiliate of the Museum of Modern Art, is named for the school that formerly occupied the building. The structure that was once known as Plant No. 7 is being converted into high-rise apartments.

 

Long Island City is home to the country’s largest fortune cookie factory, operated by Wonton Foods, which produces an astonishing four million of the sweet treats daily. It’s easy to believe that this factory produces fortunes that are the real deal, however, as evidenced by an amazing incident that took place in May of 2005, when lucky numbers included with their cookies led to 110 people across the United States winning anywhere from $100,000 to $500,000 each on a Powerball drawing. Long Island City today is a very diverse neighborhood, home to film studios, museums, public parks, and more, and it doesn’t take spending very much time in the area to see why it’s become so popular.
