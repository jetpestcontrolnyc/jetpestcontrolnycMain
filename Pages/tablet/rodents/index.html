Rodent Control

There is no hiding it; New York City has a rodent proble m . With the 2 million rats prancing around the area and earning a world-famous reputation in the City that Never Sleeps, you may be surprised to know New York is ranke d as having the third-worst rat problem in the country. The influx of rodents to homes, businesses, alleyways, subways, and the stops in between are largely due to population density, worn-down buildings, negligence in treatment, and food waste.

 

When the restaurants rodents had come to rely on shut down during the pandemic, the problem worsened significantly. The CDC warned New York City visitors and residents that rats in the area displayed “unusual or aggressive” behavior due to starvation. It got so bad, they even resorted to cannibalism. As the country is opening back up, the rodents are coming out of hiding.

 

Mice and rats are drawn anywhere that can fulfill their basic needs for shelter, food, and water. In New York, we have that in abundance. Rats can squeeze through impressively small openings, and mice, in particular, can sneak into a building even when given the tiniest of gaps, about the size of dime. They’re also able to jump as high as twelve inches, so they can find escape routes quickly if they sense anyone approaching and need to hide.

 

Rodents have excellent immune systems, so they’ll eat just about anything, which is why you’ll frequently see them scavenging around garbage cans, munching on what they can find in your kitchen or left on subway platforms. The issue isn’t just contamination of the food they touch; these animals carry and spread major diseases like Salmonella, Hantavirus, Leptospirosis, and Plague. Those can be transmitted even without directly touching the creatures. If you do come in contact and the rodent lashes out to attack, you can contract diseases like Rat-Bite Fever. 

 

That’s part of why it’s so important to let pest control experts handle rodent problems; they’re a public health threat. Mice and rats constantly chew on whatever they can find in an attempt to keep the nonstop growth of their incisors dulled down. As a result, they gnaw through wires and burrow through walls, creating fire hazards and structural damage wherever they go. They’ve even chewed through car engine wires. Mice are known to chew straight through books, furniture, and even appliances.

 

Experts like those at Jet Pest Control can do a thorough inspection and check for damage to your property, making sure to seal off entry points so new rodents can’t get in. In 2017, Mayor Bill de Blasio dedicated an extra $32 million to attack the city’s rats. It just wasn’t enough; complaints to New York’s rodent hotline surged by 80% between 2020 and 2021. At a certain point, you have to take things into your own hands and call out an expert yourself. That’s where Jet Pest Control comes in.
