MANHATTAN, NEW YORK

MANHATTAN, NY

It is nearly impossible to write about what encompasses Manhattan, affectionately known as "the city." One of the five boroughs that make up The Big Apple, Manhattan, New York, is a big city on a small island. Boasting a population of over 1.6 million on a landmass of just under 34 square miles, a third of which is made up of water, Manhattan has the highest population density in the country, with over 27,000 people per square mile.

A city that never sleeps, it is made up of 262 blocks split into three divisions known as Uptown, Midtown, and Downtown. Uptown is home to the Upper East and West Sides and Harlem and is an area with world-famous museums like The Metropolitan Museum Of Art, The Museum Of Natural History, The Studio Museum, and the Met Cloisters. Midtown includes such famous landmarks as Times Square, Grand Central Station, Rockefeller Center, and the U.N. Headquarters. Downtown has some of the more interesting neighborhoods such as Tribeca, Chelsea, Soho, Chinatown, and Little Italy.


Each neighborhood is known for its local specialties, such as the authentic restaurants and cultural shops of Chinatown and Little Italy or the high-end fashion boutiques of Soho. Chelsea is known as an artist's haven, with over 300 galleries filled with amazing art pieces. Tribeca, short for the Triangle Beneath Canal, underwent an incredible overhaul, transforming from a mostly deserted warehouse district into one of the hippest parts of the city, filled to the brim with restaurants, boutiques, bars, and galleries. For 15 years, there was a free walking tour through many of the art studios in the neighborhood, allowing for a unique behind-the-scenes look at the work and techniques of some of the best local artists available.

 

The Lenape and Wappinger Native Americans long inhabited the area that is now Manhattan, but in 1524, explorer Giovanni da Verrazano became the first documented European to visit the area. In 1626, the first director-general of New Netherland, Peter Minuet, was said to have purchased Manhattan, then a part of the American Islands, from local Native Americans for beads and trinkets worth approximately $16, perhaps the biggest heist in history. In 1664, the English took possession of the island, which had already been incorporated as the city of New Amsterdam. Renamed New York City, it quickly began to play an important role in the nation's early history and was the country's first capital. Years later, in 1898, Manhattan was combined with the newly created Brooklyn, Bronx, Queens, and Staten Island to make up the greater New York City.

 

Manhattan has a very diverse population compared to almost anywhere else in the country and was the original melting pot. For the younger generation, there are many A+ level public and private schools ready to mold students' minds into becoming the future leaders of America. Most notably, prestigious academies such as Trinity, Regis, and Dalton High Schools. 

While part of the fun of living here is the interesting people to meet and interact with everywhere you go, but it's not as fun when bugs gather similarly. There are, unfortunately, more nuisance critters than there are people. Luckily for the citizens of Manhattan and the surrounding areas, Jet Pest Control has professionals on hand ready to tackle all of your pest control needs. From our specialty bed bug treatments to rodents, fleas, roaches, and more, contact us today to discuss your options.
